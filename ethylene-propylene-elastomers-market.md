<p><p>Ethylene-Propylene Elastomers (EPM and EPDM) can be categorized into compatible and miscible types based on their blending behavior with other materials. Compatible elastomers blend well with polymers, maintaining distinct phases while improving properties like flexibility and impact resistance. Miscible elastomers, on the other hand, merge at the molecular level, creating a uniform blend that enhances overall performance. Both types are versatile and widely used in automotive, construction, and consumer goods, driven by their mechanical properties and resistance to weathering.</p></p>
