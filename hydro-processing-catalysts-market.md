<p><p>The hydro-processing catalysts market comprises two primary types: hydrotreating and hydrocracking. Hydrotreating involves removing impurities such as sulfur and nitrogen from crude oils, enhancing fuel quality and meeting regulatory standards. In contrast, hydrocracking breaks down heavier oils into lighter, more valuable products like gasoline and diesel through catalytic processes at elevated temperatures and pressures. Both methods are essential for refining operations, enabling efficient production of clean fuels and improving refinery economics while addressing environmental concerns.</p></p>
